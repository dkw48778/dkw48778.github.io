Selecting Your Flooring: Basics

There are several things to consider when you think about making decisions about your flooring. You should think about your budget but you will also need to think about some other things. You should think about your individual preferences such as do you want a warm or cool surface (carpet or hard surface). How much foot traffic will your area endure? Do you have pets or children which might lead to stains or spills? What is the likelyhood that there will be water that will come in contact with your flooring? What type of subfloor material will you be covering (concrete, plywood, particle board...). Other considerations will be mentioned further on in this page.

Advantages & Disadvantages Information

Material Cost Advantages Disadvantages
carpet: polyester $ Very stain resistant material Matting will occur in high traffic areas. The spring back value is referred to as resiliancy
carpet: nylon $$ Strongest, Most resiliant Worst of the stain resistant fibers. Stainmaster products have stain resistance built into the dye process. Scotch guard is sprayed on and will disappear after about 3 shampoos
carpet: triexta $$$ Made from corn extract. Most stain resistant Newest in the product lines and will need 10 years to show full claims
vinyl (sheet) $ Water-resistant, stain-resistant Thin and easy to puncture. Home appraisers do not see it as an upgrade
Luxury Vinyl Tile (LVT) or Luxury Vinyl Plank (LVP) $$ Realistic look. Can be put over any subfloor. Hollow sounding without including a sound barrier which adds to the cost ($0.35-$0.65/sq.ft). Material can be glued down to prevent hollow sound in some situations
Ceramic $$$ Tough. Long lasting. Solid. Waterproof. Seen as an upgrade on the value of the home. Cold. Hard underfoot. If chipped, the whole tile must be replaced because it is a clay tile with a coating. Slippery if wet.
Porcelin $$$$ Tough. Long lasting. Solid. Waterproof. Tile is consistent throughout so chips will not be as evident. Seen as an upgrade on the value of the home. Cold. Hard underfoot. Slippery if wet.
Engineered Wood $ Tough surface of aluminum oxide Softer surface than tile materials and so it can dent and scratch more easily than vinyl materials
Bamboo $$ 'Green' product. Adds to the appraisal value of the house. Softer surface than tile materials and so it can dent and scratch more easily than vinyl materials
Hardwood $$$ 'Green' product. Adds to the value of the house. Consideration must be given to the hardness of the material in high traffic areas Can be sanded and refinished to restore the floor



Other Costs

Remember to include the fact that there will be certain additional costs and there may be some unforseen costs

  • Installation, preparation: Consider whether furniture will need to be moved. Take-up and disposal of old materials.
  • Installation, floor prep: With carpet there will be padding and tack strip. If a new ceramic or porcelin area, there will be water-proof backerboard. If the sub-floor is particle board, it will have to be replaced.
  • Actual Installation: Installation is charged at a per sq.ft. price. There will be materials to attach the product such as adhesives, grout, mortar, nails, etc. (these items are not high ticket items).
